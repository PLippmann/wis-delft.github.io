SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

The workshop will accept both regular papers and short papers (work in progress, vision/outrageous ideas).

Summary papers (NEW CATEGORY): In addition to regular submissions, we also welcome 4 page papers which summarise long papers already accepted (any, maybe, presented) at major database or ML/NLP conferences or journals, as long as they are a good match for the topics. These papers will not be included in the proceedings and it should be indicated in the title of the submission that the paper is a summary paper.

All submissions must be prepared in accordance with the IEEE template available here. The workshop follows the same rules of Conflicts of Interest (COI) as ICDE 2024. The following are the page limits (excluding references):

Regular papers: 6 pages
Short papers: 4 pages

All submissions (in PDF format) should be sent to Easychair.
